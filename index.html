Coffee App


Brief: Grinding and brewing coffee makes for the freshest, tastiest brews, yet it’s sometimes difficult to know the exact amount of coffee to use for a pourover, or the precise grind you need to for a french press.

Propose a design for someone who is new to grinding and brewing their own coffee, allowing them to find their coffee, pick the intended brewing method, and walking them through the process (although this doesn’t need to be detailed in the challenge).

Research Process:

Competitive Analysis -

There are very few mobile applications that help new user to make coffee on their own from scratch. I looked at some of the applications such as SlowBar and coffee.guru. The common feature I found in these apps that they suggest the step by step method with timers for different brew types. Some of the apps do not allow a user to select the number of cups of coffee, weight conversion, roast level and grind level of coffee beans. Also, I found that none of these apps gives information about the particular coffee beans and their taste notes for intended brew type.

Brainstorm -

To find the best solution to the problem I used the brainstorming method by gathering friends. I asked them questions such as

  • What do they expect from the app?
  • How do they want to navigate through the app?
  • Which feature is important for them

Conceptual Model -

Based upon the answers in the Brainstorming session, I created a conceptual model for the best solution. I used Flow Chart diagram to show the possible navigation of the application.

Design Process:

Low-Fidelity Sketches -

After my research, I worked on low fidelity sketches. After feedback from friends and few iterations, I finalized sketches to design the app.
